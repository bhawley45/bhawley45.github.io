Pigs?

A commical 2D platformer

Unity Action/Adventure 2D-Platformer Pixel Art

  Embark on an epic adventure in this thrilling 2D platformer where you, a valiant young prince, must save your kingdom from a mysterious foe!

  As dawn breaks, you awaken in the throne room to an eerie silence, strange noises echoing through the halls. Investigating further you discover something truly horrifying: pig soldiers roaming freely, traps laid in peculiar ways, and the kingdom's precious treasures scattered upon the floor! With the fate of your people resting on your shoulders, you embark on a perilous battle throughout the castle, reclaiming what is rightfully yours.

  Navigate through questionable level designs, dodge exotic trap placements, and overcome artistic choices that challenge your senses as you journey through the heart of the castle :)

Will the oinks of tyranny echo through the land forever?

Unity (2022.3.13f1)

How it was made...


Art Assets:

  This project primarily uses assets from the "Kings and Pigs" pack from itch.io. After doing some staging for the levels though I decided to add some custom sprites and animations to help blend the environment. I used GIMP, which I was fairly new to at the time, but the challenge of matching the art style of the original pack was a fun learning experience.


Game Development:

  This project is my second time using Unity for a "full" game project. The game itself is definitely rough around the edges but served as a stepping stone in my game dev journey. Development was fairly straightforward, including blocking levels, asset design and implementation, and scripting. However, I had to learn some previously unknown features of Unity to fit my vision for the game, most notably: sprite maps, custom UI elements, sound effects, and the Cinemachine state-driven camera system.


Challenges & Learning:

  The hardest challenge encountered was trying to use Unity's Cinemachine camera system, which I was able to improve in subsequent projects. The only other hurdle, as stated earlier, was creating pixel art in a similar style to the original asset pack.
