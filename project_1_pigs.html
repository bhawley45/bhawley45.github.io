Pigs?

A commical 2D platformer

Unity Action/Adventure 2D-Platformer Pixel Art

  Embark on an epic adventure in this thrilling 2D platformer where you, a valiant young prince, must save your kingdom from a mysterious foe!

  As dawn breaks, you awaken in the throne room to an eerie silence, strange noises echoing through the halls. Investigating further you discover something truly horrifying: pig soldiers roaming freely, traps laid in peculiar ways, and the kingdom's precious treasures scattered upon the floor! With the fate of your people resting on your shoulders, you embark on a perilous battle throughout the castle, reclaiming what is rightfully yours.

  Navigate through questionable level designs, dodge exotic trap placements, and overcome artistic choices that challenge your senses as you journey through the heart of the castle :)

  Will you emerge victorious and restore peace to your homeland, or will the oinks of tyranny echo through the land forever?

How it was made...

  Game Engine: Unity (2022.3.13f1)

  Art Assets: This project primarily uses assets from the "Kings and Pigs" pack on itch.io, giving a fairly cohesive foundation for most of the game. I felt like it was still missing something while staging the levels though so I added some custom sprites, created in GIMP, an open-source image editing suite. I was fairly new to GIMP at the time but the challenge of matching the artistic styling of the original pack was a fun learning experience.

  Game Development: This project was my second time using unity for a "full" project, it's definitely a little rough around the edges but it's a step in my game dev journey none-the-less. "Pigs?" served as an excellent learning opportunity, introducing me to several new aspects of Unity that were crucial for it to be fully realized. From spritemaps, custom UI elements, sound effects, and Cinemachine state-driven camera systems.

  Challenges and Learning: Most elements were pretty straight forward, but I struggled quite a bit with the camera systems. I was able to improve significantly in my next project. The only other major hurdle as learning how to use GIMP and match the style of the pre-made asset pack I was using as a foundation.
